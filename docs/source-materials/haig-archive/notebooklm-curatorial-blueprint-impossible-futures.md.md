Rehearsing Impossible Futures

This concept should become one of the primary interpretive frames for the site.

#### Definition:

Rehearsing impossible futures is the practice of writing the fiction of a future system first, then reverse-engineering the spatial, technical, social, sonic, and bureaucratic infrastructure required to make that fiction survivable.

#### Core principles:

Reject forecasting in favor of rehearsal.

Prototype futures in low-risk environments such as stage, code, screen, score, model, or installation.

Treat stories as engineering tools, not escapism.

Create diegetic prototypes: artifacts that appear to belong to a world that does not yet exist.

Use repetition and rehearsal to stabilize speculative realities until they become navigable.

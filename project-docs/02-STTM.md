# Space Time Tuning Machine
## AI-Augmented Immersive Performance System

**Tier:** Lead Project  
**Type:** Diegetic Prototype / Live Performance System  
**Status:** Active / Performing  
**Validated by:** Sundance Institute · The Broad Museum · Joe's Pub · SXSW · Metropolitan Museum of Art  

---

## The Question

Most instruments play notes. The Space Time Tuning Machine plays the space between the note and the memory of the note — the gap where the machine stops and the body begins.

STTM is a hybrid instrument built from scrap electronics, hacked vintage synthesizers, and custom software that uses ambient noise, violin resonance, and somatic gesture as inputs to a generative AI system that produces sound the performer could not have made alone.

The name is precise. It is a machine for tuning space and time — not the space of a room, but the space inside a displaced memory. Not the time of a clock, but the time of a crane migrating south, carrying something it cannot name toward somewhere that may no longer exist.

---

## Concept: The Instrument as Diegetic Prototype

STTM is framed as a **diegetic prototype** — a functional prop from a future that hasn't arrived yet. Not a finished product, but a working argument: that AI-integrated musical instruments are not hypothetical, and that the human role in them is not to program them but to perform with them — to improvise against, alongside, and through their generative output.

The violin is acoustically complete. It can already do everything a violin can do. What it cannot do alone is remember — it cannot hold the texture of a grandmother's kitchen in Bourj Hammoud while producing sound in a concert hall in Los Angeles. It cannot hold both the Armenian folk song "Groong" (crane) about a dislocated wanderer and the frequency signature of the city outside the venue window. STTM was built to make that holding possible.

## Technical Architecture

| Layer | Component | Function |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **Physical Input** | Custom hardware, hacked electronics, contact microphones, Raspberry Pi | Captures violin resonance, ambient room acoustics, and performer gesture as raw signal. Circuitry includes intentional noise and deliberate imprecision. |
| **Signal Processing** | Max/MSP signal chain, custom patches | Transforms acoustic input into control signals for the generative layer. Gesture becomes parameter. |
| **Generative Layer** | AI inference (Suno/custom models), DERIVE memory architecture | Receives performer signals and archive queries simultaneously. Produces generative audio responses that react to what is being played in real time. The machine improvises back. |
| **Output Synthesis** | Ableton Live, MIDI/OSC routing, speaker system | Merges live violin and AI-generated audio into a single spatial field. The audience cannot cleanly separate what the human made from what the machine made. This is intentional. |

## Narrative Foundation: Groong

STTM's conceptual core is the Armenian folk song *Groong* — a lament about a crane in migration, separated from its flock, searching for a home it carries only as memory. The crane is the diaspora's animal: it migrates not by choice but by displacement, and it navigates by instinct rather than map.

The instrument is built around this image structurally:

- The ambient noise it captures from the performance space is **the environment the crane finds itself in**
- The violin is **the crane's call** — the thing that identifies it as itself
- The generative AI is **the flock it no longer has** — producing responses that are recognizable as related but not identical, familiar but always slightly foreign
- The performance is **the flight**

## The Human Role: Conducting vs. Playing

STTM represents a fundamental reframing of what a performer does. The violinist does not play the AI — they conduct it.

A conductor does not produce the sound themselves, but their judgment, timing, and cultural interpretation determine whether the ensemble produces something true. The performer's role is curation under pressure: in real time, in front of an audience, deciding which generative outputs to accept, which to redirect, and which to allow to decay into silence.

| | Relationship | Skill | Output |
|--|------------|-------|--------|
| **Traditional Instrument** | Player → Sound | Direct. Skill = control. | Bounded by technique. |
| **STTM** | Player ↔ System → Sound | Dialogic. Skill = responsiveness. | Emerges from conversation. |

## Evolution: STTM → DERIVE

STTM's 2025 evolution is DERIVE. Where STTM emphasized the physical — a hybrid instrument performing live — DERIVE abstracts and extends this into a full generative pipeline: a personal archive embedded in latent space, navigable by emotional adjacency, producing artifacts across audio, text, and image.

The relationship is not replacement but maturation. STTM is still the live performance interface; DERIVE is the archive it performs from.

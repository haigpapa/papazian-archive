# TEBR
## AI-Human Generative Sonic Universe

**Status:** Active Development

## The Hypothesis

Every diasporic archive is a broken instrument. The strings are still there — the tuning is not. TEBR is the attempt to play it anyway.

Where DERIVE treats displacement as a retrieval problem — building an engine to navigate fractured memory — TEBR treats it as a **compositional premise**. The question is not *how do we remember?* but *what do we build from what remains?*

The answer is a sonic universe: not nostalgic reconstruction, not Western assimilation, but a third territory — **diasporic futurism** — assembled from glitch, ancestral fragment, and algorithmic drift.

> *"The past and future belong to the diasporans, not the past." — Krikor Beledian. TEBR operationalizes the second half of this sentence.*

## System Architecture

| Layer | Function | Materials |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| **Ancestral Corpus** | Raw material — the pre-digital, pre-displacement archive. Field recordings, oral history fragments, Armenian liturgical melodic structures, Levantine maqam patterns, family phonetics. | Voice memos, legacy cassette transfers, musical scores, language recordings |
| **Glitch Layer** | Deliberate corruption and mis-translation. AI models are fed culturally specific prompts designed to produce instructive failure — the errors are not discarded but harvested as compositional material. | Suno v4/v5, Udio, custom adversarial prompting protocols |
| **Synthesis Engine** | Human curation of the collision. The composer does not author; they select — choosing which hallucinations resonate, which corruptions ring true, which errors accidentally tell the truth. | Ableton Live, Max/MSP, DERIVE memory architecture as retrieval backbone |

---

## Why Glitch Is Not Failure

Standard generative audio pipelines treat model errors as noise to be minimized. TEBR inverts this.

When Suno defaults from Lebanese dialect to Egyptian Arabic, or collapses a maqam quarter-tone into a Western semitone, the model is not failing — it is revealing the exact shape of the bias embedded in its training data. That shape is culturally specific. It is evidence.

TEBR collects this evidence and turns it into material. **The glitch is the gap between what the machine knows and what the diaspora knows.** That gap is the aesthetic territory. The project lives in it deliberately, refusing resolution in either direction — neither the comfort of Western coherence nor the paralysis of unmediated nostalgia.

---

## Diasporic Futurism — Working Definition

The practice of constructing possible futures from fragments of displaced pasts, using the collision between ancestral memory and algorithmic systems as generative friction rather than obstacle.

## Relationship to Other Systems

- **DERIVE** supplies the memory architecture for archive retrieval
- **HAH-WAS** acts as quality control — distinguishing productive hallucination from damaging erasure
- **STTM** provides the live performance interface — TEBR artifacts are the score; STTM is the instrument

---

## Output Formats

| Format | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Sonic Artifacts** | Generated tracks — not finished songs. Fragments of 45–180 seconds that resist resolution. Organized into a taxonomy by cultural origin, glitch type, and emotional register. |
| **Performance Scores** | Notation systems (conventional and invented) that specify the relationship between pre-generated material and live improvisation. The score is a protocol, not a fixed text. |
| **Process Documentation** | Adversarial prompt logs, failure taxonomies, and model behavior records that constitute the research layer. This documentation is itself a form of publication. |

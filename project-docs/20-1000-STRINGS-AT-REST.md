# 1000 Strings at Rest / This Song Is Not Available in Exile
## Multimedia Sound Sculpture Installation

**Tier:** Archive  
**Type:** Sound Art / Multimedia Installation  
**Year:** 2022  
**Location:** Los Angeles, October 2022  
**Venue:** The Broad Museum (related STTM performance, September 2022)  
**Context:** Created concurrent with Mashrou' Leila's final West Coast appearances  

---

## The Work

*1000 Strings at Rest* is a sound sculpture installation that addresses displacement, memory, and the condition of music in exile — specifically the condition of music that cannot be heard in the places that made it.

The subtitle — *This Song Is Not Available in Exile* — is both a statement and a system error. It describes the experience of trying to stream a song in a country where the platform has geo-blocked it, or trying to remember a melody that was learned in a language you no longer have full access to. The song exists. You cannot reach it. The unavailability is not absence — it is a form of presence defined by obstruction.

---

## The Image: Strings at Rest

A string instrument at rest is not silent. The strings hold tension without vibration — they are charged with potential sound, waiting for the bow or the pluck that would release it. The instrument is ready. The music is latent.

*1000 Strings at Rest* plays with this condition: what does it mean for a thousand strings to be present and silent simultaneously? The installation constructs an acoustic space in which latent sound is the primary material — the potential of music that has not yet been, or can no longer be, released.

This is a spatial argument about exile: the displaced person does not lack culture. They carry it. It is present, held under tension, unavailable for release in the specific acoustic and social conditions that would give it meaning. The strings are not broken. The music is not gone. It is waiting for a room that no longer exists.

---

## Context: The Final Year

*1000 Strings at Rest* was created in 2022, the year Mashrou' Leila performed its final concerts and announced its dissolution. The West Coast run — including the Broad Museum performance in September — marked the end of a 14-year period of making music that was simultaneously globally distributed and locally banned.

The installation exists in relation to this context without being reducible to it. It is not a monument to the band. It is an investigation of a condition that the band's history made newly legible: what happens to music when the places that made it refuse to hear it?

---

## Relationship to STTM and DERIVE

The installation shares material concerns with both STTM and DERIVE, and was created in the period that bridges them:

- **From STTM:** The violin as acoustic tether to physical presence; the instrument as memory technology
- **Toward DERIVE:** The archive as a system of latent sounds waiting for retrieval conditions that may never come; the question of what it means to hold cultural material in suspension

*1000 Strings at Rest* is the installation form of the question that DERIVE became a computational answer to: how do you hold fragmented cultural memory in a form that preserves its potential without forcing it into premature coherence?

---

## The Title's Double Meaning

**1000 Strings at Rest** — the physical image of stringed instruments, held and silent, present and unavailable.

**This Song Is Not Available in Exile** — the geo-block error message as a description of a condition; the platform's technical language accidentally naming something true about the experience of displacement.

Both titles are doing the same work: making the obstruction visible as a form, rather than treating it as a problem to be solved.

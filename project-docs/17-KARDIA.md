# Kardia
## Somatic Biofeedback Synthesizer

**Tier:** Secondary Project  
**Type:** Emotional Operating System  
**Status:** Prototype / Development  
**Input:** Heart Rate Variability (HRV)  
**Hardware:** Compatible with Polar H10, Apple Watch, Garmin HRV sensors  

---

## The Premise

The heart does not keep time like a metronome. Between each beat, the interval varies — slightly, continuously, in response to breath, attention, emotion, and stress. This variation — Heart Rate Variability, HRV — is the body's most legible signal of its own emotional state.

High HRV indicates regulated, adaptive nervous system function. Low HRV indicates stress, depletion, or threat response. The cardiologist reads it as a health metric.

**Kardia reads it as a score.**

Kardia is a biofeedback synthesizer that translates real-time HRV data into personalized acoustic soundscapes. The body composes. The system performs. The human listener hears, in real time, what their nervous system is doing — not as a graph or a number, but as sound with texture, rhythm, and emotional register that mirrors the physiological state generating it.

---

## Signal Chain

| HRV Dimension | Sonic Parameter | Physiological Logic |
|--------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| **HRV Mean (RMSSD)** | Harmonic density / richness | High HRV = rich, complex harmonic content. Low HRV = sparse, thin texture. The body's resilience is audible as musical fullness. |
| **Inter-Beat Variation** | Rhythmic irregularity / swing | The natural variation between beats becomes the rhythmic feel of the soundscape. High variation = loose, organic feel. Low variation = mechanical, driven. |
| **Breathing Rate (derived)** | Phrase length / phrasing breath | The respiratory rate extracted from HRV patterns determines how long melodic phrases extend before resolving or suspending. |
| **Stress Index** | Dissonance level / tension | Elevated stress markers introduce controlled dissonance — the soundscape becomes more tense, less resolved, until the physiological state shifts. |
| **Recovery Gradient** | Harmonic resolution / arrival | As HRV improves, the soundscape moves toward resolution — harmonics settle, rhythm regularizes, the music lands. Recovery is audible as arrival. |

## The Therapeutic Argument

Standard biofeedback interfaces present physiological data visually — a number, a graph, a colored indicator. The limitation of visual biofeedback is cognitive: you have to read the data, interpret it, and then decide what to do with it. This involves cortical processing that itself affects the physiological state you're trying to regulate.

Kardia's hypothesis is that **auditory biofeedback bypasses this interpretive step.** The soundscape is not a representation of your physiological state that you must decode — it is a direct acoustic expression of it, processed by a sensory system that has been responding to environmental sound signals for 300 million years without requiring conscious interpretation.

The nervous system responds to the soundscape before the mind decides whether to. This creates a genuine feedback loop:

```
Body → HRV signal → Kardia → Soundscape → Body → HRV signal
```

The loop stabilizes toward the physiological state the soundscape is designed to encourage.

> *Most wellness technology tells you how stressed you are. Kardia makes stress audible — not as alarm or alert, but as music. The difference between hearing a number and hearing a sound is the difference between being informed and being affected.*

## Applications

**Clinical and therapeutic contexts.** Biofeedback-assisted regulation for anxiety, PTSD, and stress-related conditions. The soundscape functions as a non-verbal, non-pharmaceutical intervention that does not require the patient to consciously engage with data.

**Performance preparation.** Musicians, athletes, and public speakers using Kardia pre-performance to audibly monitor and regulate arousal state. The performer hears their own readiness.

**Immersive installation.** Gallery or public installation context where visitors' collective HRV generates a shared soundscape, making the emotional state of a group visible (audible) as a communal experience. A room that sounds like the people inside it.

**Compositional research.** Using HRV-generated soundscapes as raw material for composition — treating the body's signal as a found sound source with genuine emotional content that no composer could fabricate.

## Connection to the Larger System

Kardia sits at the intersection of two persistent concerns in the portfolio: the primacy of the body as a signal source (embodiment over abstraction, as in 3D-Beat-Synth and STTM) and the use of acoustic output as a primary medium for making invisible states legible (as in SUNBURN and DERIVE).

Where STTM uses gesture to control generative sound, Kardia uses autonomic physiology. The body as instrument, but operating below the threshold of conscious control — the improvisation happening in the nervous system, not the hands.

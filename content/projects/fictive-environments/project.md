# FICTIVE ENVIRONMENTS
 
## Thesis
Fiction can do more than describe another world; it can specify the structures required to build one.
 
## Short Description
Fictive Environments is Haig Papazian’s studio practice for developing spatial, computational, narrative, and civic projects from shared systems thinking.
 
## Full Description
The studio begins by writing the future condition clearly enough to test: a residency that behaves as care infrastructure, an archive organized by relation, or an interface that preserves cultural specificity. It then works backward toward the spatial briefs, software, partnerships, protocols, and public language needed to make that condition operational. Systems Choreography is the method; Fictive Environments is the practice through which that method is deployed.
 
## Highlights
- Cinematic Rail Type: 10-card studio OS rail
- Outputs: spatial briefs, software systems, narrative environments, and cultural programs
- Method: structural fiction followed by prototyping and deployment

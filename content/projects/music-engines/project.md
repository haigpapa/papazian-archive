# Music Engines

## Thesis
The backend listening system that turns audio research intuition into repeatable infrastructure.

## Short Description
Music Engines powers analysis pipelines for HAH-WAS, DERIVE, TEBR, and the broader AI music research stack.

## Full Description
The system extracts features, transcribes, clusters, and persists audio knowledge so larger cultural and generative questions can be asked at scale. It is the quiet engine behind the more visible music-AI systems: less a product than a listening architecture.

## Highlights
- Async audio analysis infrastructure for AI music research
- Supports HAH-WAS, DERIVE, and TEBR pipelines
- Built around feature extraction, transcription, embeddings, and graph-like querying
- A technical proof of the portfolio methodology at backend scale

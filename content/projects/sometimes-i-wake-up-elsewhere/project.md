# SOMETIMES I WAKE UP ELSEWHERE
 
## Thesis
Exile changes not only where a story happens, but how memory permits the story to be entered and returned to.
 
## Short Description
Sometimes I Wake Up Elsewhere is a 28,000-word autofiction manuscript being developed as a nonlinear reading environment of rooms, returns, and altered states.
 
## Full Description
Across approximately 100 modular entries, ordinary rooms, errands, animals, forms, and physical details accumulate into a dream logic of displacement. The digital structure does not merely illustrate the manuscript: revisiting a passage can change its state, and accumulated “temporal debt” can alter the reading surface. The manuscript exists; the Möbius Engine and interface are active development layers. The project remains literary first, with the system designed to protect the tenderness, humor, and instability of the writing.
 
## Highlights
- Cinematic Rail Type: 16-card flagship dossier
- Form: approximately 28,000 words across a modular 100-entry architecture
- Method: nonlinear return, state change, temporal debt, and typographic decay
- Status: manuscript active; playable interface and walkthrough remain in development

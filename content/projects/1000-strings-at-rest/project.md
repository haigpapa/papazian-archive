# 1000 Strings at Rest

## Thesis
A silent field of tension where strings, bodies, distance, and memory remain suspended before performance.

## Short Description
1000 Strings at Rest bridges Mashrou Leila, STTM, and later spatial sound work through the image of instruments held in unresolved tension.

## Full Description
Even as an archive-scale work, it carries unusual emotional and methodological weight. String, body, resonance, and installation are held in suspension rather than released into spectacle. The work becomes a quiet connective tissue between public performance and the later instruments of memory.

## Highlights
- Links Mashrou Leila, STTM, and DERIVE
- Treats strings as spatial and emotional infrastructure
- Archive entry with disproportionate conceptual weight
- Useful hinge between performance and installation work

Papazian Archive: Website Structural Brief & Copy Matrix
This document outlines the architectural logic and content strategy for the Papazian Archive digital interface. It serves as a blueprint for translating the master project archive and curatorial blueprints into a functional, multi-modal web experience.
Executive Summary: Design Architecture
The Papazian Archive is structured around five distinct systems of meaning, designed to provide both linear narrative and non-linear exploration of the artist's body of work.
	•	Orbit (Home): The central gravitational point of the archive. This mode acts as a dynamic entry point, visualizing the current state of the entire system and allowing users to initiate a trajectory into specific worlds or themes.
	•	Works (Curated Spine): A curated chronological journey. This mode provides a focused, narrative-driven experience through the 15 primary projects, organized by System Worlds to demonstrate the evolution of the artist’s practice.
	•	Index (Unwrapped Grid): A holistic, non-hierarchical view. This system unwraps the archive into a high-density grid, prioritizing rapid browsing, filtering by media type, and visual comparison across different worlds.
	•	Atlas (Relations): A conceptual mapping mode. The Atlas visualizes the connective tissue between projects, highlighting recurring system tags, shared technical methodologies, and philosophical threads that transcend chronological order.
	•	Essays (Writing Panel): The critical layer. A dedicated panel for long-form writing and theoretical inquiry, providing deep context and academic grounding for the works presented in the other modes.
